IPE Example


Snap Modes



I. Find the midpoint of a straight line segment

1. Draw the line
2. Select Snap to control point

3. Select a marker and hover on the line.
4. At the midpoint a small square appears. Click on it. The marker would be settled on the mid point of the line.


Application: Find the centroid of a triangle. (Draw the segments separately)

II. Draw a Perpendicular to a straight line at its midpoint.
1. First mark the midpoint. Zoom in to that point.
2. Enable angular snap
3. Hover on top of the mid point. Press F1. The cursored point is selected as the new origin of the coordinate system.
4. Take the cursor to rest on any other point on the line (do not worry about the accompanying star. The tip of the cursor must be placed at the proper point; the star would follow suit) and Press F2. This selects the new base direction of the coordinate system. The new coordinate is shown with green lines.

5. Now you can easily put a marker on the green perpendicular line, Press Ctrl + F2 to revert to the original system of axes, Ctrl + F1 to hide the green lines and then join the two markers after enabling snap to vertex (F4)
6. To erase the markers afterwards, select them by drawing selection rectangles around them, pointing won't work (it would select the line instead) and pressing DEL.
